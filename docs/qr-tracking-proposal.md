## What you'd get

Every QR code you put out in the world points to a short URL on your own site — something like `drjenniferwilson.com/go/card` or `drjenniferwilson.com/go/window`. When someone scans it, their phone opens that URL, which quietly tracks the scan and then instantly forwards them to wherever you want — usually your SimplePractice booking page or a specific Intensive.

You get a single dashboard showing:

- How many people scanned each QR code
- When they scanned (time of day, day of week, trends over time)
- General location (country/region — nothing personal)
- Which QR codes are actually working and which ones aren't

This means you can make real decisions. If the business card QR is getting 40 scans a week and the window sign is getting 2, you know where to focus. If you run a print mailer and track it to `drjenniferwilson.com/go/spring-mailer`, you'll know exactly how many people it reached — something mailers usually can't tell you.

**Option A — Canva (recommended if you already use it for designs):**

1. Open your Canva design (business card, flyer, etc.)
2. In the left sidebar, click **Elements** → search **"QR code"**
3. Click the QR Code generator element
4. Paste `https://drjenniferwilson.com/go/card` into the URL field
5. The QR code appears in your design instantly — drag it to where you want it, resize it, match your brand colors if you want
6. Done. You can export and print immediately.

**Option B — Free web-based QR generator:**

If you're not in Canva, there are dozens of free, no-signup-needed QR generators. Two solid ones:

- **qr-code-generator.com** — paste URL, download PNG
- **QRCode Monkey** — paste URL, customize colors/logo, download high-res PNG

Paste the URL → click Generate → download the PNG file → use it wherever you need it.

**Option C — I make it for you:**

If you'd rather not, I can also just generate the PNG and email it to you. Takes me another minute after creating the URL. Your call either way — whichever fits your workflow. Most designers find Canva is faster than waiting for someone else, but I'm happy to do it either way.

### 4. You use the QR code in your design

Drop it into a business card, flyer, window sign, email signature, Instagram highlight cover, whatever. Print, post, publish.

### 5. You watch the dashboard

Every scan shows up. You can log in and see the numbers any time.

## A note on QR code design

When you generate a QR code (in Canva or any generator), you'll notice most tools let you:

- Change the color to match your brand (sage green, cream background, etc.)
- Embed a small logo in the center (your lotus logo, for instance)
- Customize the corner "eye" shapes from plain squares to rounded or circle
- Adjust error correction level (higher = more forgiving if the code is partially obscured)

A word of caution: brand-customized QR codes are beautiful but can be harder for phones to scan if you go too far. Stick to the defaults from Canva/QRCode Monkey and they'll work 99% of the time. If you want a highly custom look, test it by scanning with your own phone before you print 500 business cards. I can also help review a QR design before you commit to printing.

## Privacy and HIPAA — the important part

As a licensed therapist, I'm being deliberate about what analytics tool we use. Google Analytics is a bad fit for therapy practices because Google collects a lot of personal data and doesn't sign the privacy agreements that healthcare providers need. Even though a QR scan itself isn't medical information, having Google's tracking on your site creates unnecessary exposure.

Instead, I recommend a service called **Plausible Analytics**, which is specifically designed for privacy-conscious businesses:

- **No cookies.** Nothing stored on visitors' devices.
- **No personal information collected.** No names, emails, IP addresses, or anything that could identify a patient.
- **No cross-site tracking.** Plausible only sees what happens on your site, nothing else.
- **Transparent, simple dashboard.** One page, shows you what you need.
- **GDPR / CCPA / HIPAA-friendly by design.** Because it collects nothing regulated, there's nothing to regulate.

Plausible is what companies like DuckDuckGo, Laravel, Framer, and many healthcare sites use. It's a well-established, ethical alternative to Google Analytics.

**Cost: $9/month** (first 10,000 visits/month — way more than you'll need). No contract. Cancel anytime. First 30 days are free to try.

There's a slightly fancier alternative called **Fathom for $14/month** if you prefer — functionally identical, just a different company.
