# Jane App Setup Guide — Dr. Jennifer Wilson

**Audience:** Rich (configuring Jane on Jen's behalf), Jen (if she wants to see what's happening), future Claude sessions.
**Goal:** Get Jen's Jane account configured so that every first-time client has a warm, frictionless booking experience — telehealth link auto-delivered, intake form auto-attached, and the home address never visible.
**Last updated:** 2026-04-15

---

## One constraint to know up front

**Jane does not allow guest booking.** Every first-time patient must create a Jane account (username + password) as part of their first online booking. This is not a toggle — it's how Jane's model works, because the account is what gives clients access to their intake form, telehealth join link, rescheduling, superbills, and future visits.

The best we can do is make this as smooth as possible:

1. Account creation happens **inline during booking**, not as a separate step — patients experience it as one flow, not two.
2. The `/book/index.html` page on `drjenniferwilson.com` now includes a small line of warm expectation-setting copy so patients know the account step is coming before they land on Jane. (That change shipped with this doc.)
3. Jen's branding (forest primary, sage accent, lotus logo, background photo) carries through every client-facing Jane page, so the account-creation screen still feels like *her* practice.

---

## Suggested execution order

Work through these in order. Each step builds on the one before.

1. **Hide the home address from emails and the booking page** (Section 1) — stop the leak first
2. **Configure the consultation session as an Online Appointment** (Section 2) — so Jane generates the telehealth link
3. **Build the intake form in Manual mode, then flip to Automatic** (Section 3)
4. **Paste the ready-to-use Thanks for Booking copy into Jane's settings** (Section 4)
5. **Run a test booking on yourself end-to-end** (Section 5) — confirm no address leak, telehealth link arrives, intake form is attached

## Section 1 — Hide Jen's home address from all client-facing surfaces

This fixes the issue you hit when Jane sent a test confirmation containing Jen's home address.

### Step-by-step

1. Open **Settings → Locations**
2. Click **Edit** on Jen's location
3. Scroll to the **Location Address & Map** section
4. **Uncheck** the box labeled *"Display location address on your online booking site and emails"*
5. Click **Save**

### What this does

- The Thanks for Booking email stops showing any address
- The public booking site stops showing any address
- **Billing documents (superbills, receipts) are intentionally untouched** — they still pull the real location address, which is what insurance companies expect on a superbill for reimbursement. That address only appears on the financial documents a client actively downloads, never on the booking or email surface the general public sees.

### Verify

Do this verification after you finish Section 2, by making a real test booking against your own email.

- [ ] Thanks for Booking email contains no street address
- [ ] Public `drjenniferwilson.janeapp.com` page shows no street address

### If Jen ever wants to hide the address from billing documents too

This is a separate, bigger decision — not the concern today. The cleanest path when the time comes is for her to get a UPS Store mailbox or P.O. Box (~$15/mo) that becomes her real practice mailing address, and then use Jane's **Settings → Locations → Edit → Billing Information → "Use my Location Address For Billing"** toggle to point billing at the new address. That protects her home on superbills without risking insurance rejecting the document. Revisit this only if it becomes an actual concern.

## Section 2 — Set up the consultation as a Jane Telehealth session

The secure video link is auto-generated by Jane, but only when the session is typed as an **Online Appointment**. If the session is set up as a regular in-person session, no video link attaches to the confirmation email.

### Step-by-step

1. Open **Settings → Sessions, Classes & Group Appointments**
2. Click **Edit** on the existing 15-minute consultation session (or **New Session** if you want to rebuild it clean)
3. In the session-type dropdown near the top, change the type to **Online Appointment**
4. Confirm **Location** is Jen's location (the one where the address is now hidden per Section 1)
5. Confirm the name, price, and duration are correct
6. Scroll down and check **Display in Online Booking**
7. Click **Save**
8. Sanity check: go to **Settings → Online Booking** and confirm the clinic-wide **Online Booking Policy** is set to **Allowed**

### What happens after this

When a patient books the consultation, Jane automatically:

1. Generates a unique video link for that appointment
2. Includes the link inside the **Thanks for Booking** confirmation email at the moment of booking
3. Sends a second reminder email **30 minutes before** the appointment, again containing the join link
4. Shows the same join link inside the patient's Jane account and in Jen's staff view

Jen and the patient both join from their respective links. No Zoom or Google Meet account is required on either side.

## Section 3 — Auto-send the intake form with the first appointment

Important ordering detail buried in the Jane docs: **intake forms are not sent on account creation, and not sent with the Welcome email.** They are sent only when a patient books an eligible appointment. The form link is embedded directly inside the Thanks for Booking email for that appointment.

This is actually what we want for Jen's flow: patient books the 15-minute consultation → intake form arrives in the same email → patient completes it before the call.

### Step-by-step

1. Open **Settings → Forms & Surveys**
2. On the **Intake Forms** row, click **View Forms**
3. Click **New Intake Form** (top right)
4. Work through the tabs in order:
   - **General tab**
     - Name: `New Client Intake`
     - Mode: start with **Manual** while you're building (flip to Automatic after step 5 below)
     - Validity period: however long Jen wants the form to count as "current" before a client has to redo it (90 or 180 days is common)
   - **Appointment Type tab** (this tab only appears when mode is Automatic — ignore for now, come back after step 5)
   - **Profile Fields tab** — pick which demographic/contact fields the form collects (name, DOB, phone, email, address, emergency contact, etc.)
   - **Credit Cards tab** — skip unless Jen is running Jane Payments
   - **Insurance Information tab** — skip (she's private pay + superbill)
   - **Questionnaires tab** — add Jen's clinical intake questions. Use Jane's Chart Template builder or paste items directly.
   - **Consents tab** — add the consents Jen requires (informed consent, telehealth consent, financial agreement, HIPAA notice, etc.) with signature fields
5. Click **Save Intake Form**
6. **Test by manually sending the form to yourself from a test client profile.** Read it top to bottom. Make sure every question is phrased the way Jen would phrase it. Fix anything that reads wrong.
7. Once you're happy, go back into the form, switch the **General tab** mode to **Automatic**, then switch to the **Appointment Type tab** and scope it. Recommended scope:
   - **A Specific Discipline** (Jen's discipline) — covers every appointment type under her practice
   - OR **Specific Treatments** (the 15-min consultation + first full session) if you want to be narrower
8. **Save** again

### What happens after this

- Every new client who books an eligible appointment gets the intake form link embedded in their Thanks for Booking email
- Jane tracks whether they've completed it — Jen sees the status in the staff view before the session starts
- Returning clients (who already have a valid-period form on file) won't be re-sent the form unless the validity period has expired

## Section 4 — Customize the Thanks for Booking email (with ready-to-paste copy)

Jane does not have a single "edit this email" screen. The Thanks for Booking email is assembled from **five different fields scattered across settings**. To customize it, you edit each field. Below is the full map plus ready-to-paste copy written in Jen's voice.

### 4a. The field map

| What you're changing | Where it lives in Jane |
|---|---|
| Per-service instructions (the biggest, most custom block) | Settings → Treatments, Classes & Group Appointments → Edit → **Booking Information (After Booking)** |
| Global welcome body | Settings → Language → **Thanks for Booking Email: Details** |
| Location name + description | Settings → Locations → Edit |
| Cancellation policy text (appears in all appointment emails) | Settings → Language → **Email: Cancellation Policy** |
| Logo + branding (emails, receipts, booking page, My Account) | Settings → Branding |

A few gotchas:

- The **Booking Information (After Booking)** field shows up in both the Thanks for Booking email *and* the appointment reminder email, so write it so it reads right in both contexts.
- You cannot customize the email subject line or the sender name beyond the clinic-wide email setting.
- The cancellation policy shows in every appointment-related email as long as cancellations are enabled in **Settings → Online Booking** — there's no per-email suppression.

### 4b. Ready-to-paste copy — Thanks for Booking Email: Details (global body)

**Where:** Settings → Language → **Thanks for Booking Email: Details**

```
Welcome, and thank you for making space for this. Your consultation is on the calendar. Below you'll find the details — when we'll meet, your secure video link, and your intake form. I'm glad you're here.
```

### 4c. Ready-to-paste copy — Booking Information (After Booking) for the 15-min consultation

**Where:** Settings → Treatments, Classes & Group Appointments → Edit the 15-min consultation → **Booking Information (After Booking)**

```
I look forward to our conversation. A few minutes before our call, you'll receive a reminder with your secure video link — one click to join, from any device.

Your intake form is included with this confirmation. Take a few quiet minutes to complete it ahead of our call, so our time together can stay with you and what's bringing you here.

Come as you are — with your questions, your hopes, whatever feels present. I'll be ready.

— Jen
```

### 4d. Ready-to-paste copy — Email: Cancellation Policy (global)

**Where:** Settings → Language → **Email: Cancellation Policy**

```
When plans need to shift, please give me at least twenty-four hours of notice. This creates space for another client who's waiting for a time to open up. Thank you for your care and consideration.
```

> **Note for Rich to confirm with Jen:** Does she want to charge a late-cancel or no-show fee, or does she prefer to keep the policy warm and fee-free? The default copy above is fee-free. If she wants a fee, add one more sentence — something like: *"A full session fee is kindly requested for cancellations inside that window."*

### 4e. Logo + branding

**Where:** Settings → Branding

Upload Jen's lotus logo (`logo-exports/lotus-logo.svg` or a PNG version). This appears on every client-facing email, receipt/invoice, the online booking page, and the My Account page. Confirm the colors on the branding screen match forest primary + sage accent (the same palette as her site).

### 4f. Location name + description

**Where:** Settings → Locations → Edit Jen's location

- **Location Name:** `Conscious Practice` (matches her brand name on the site)
- **Short Description:** `Dr. Jennifer Wilson, PhD, LCSW — Florida-based therapy, in-person and telehealth.`
- **Long Description:** leave blank, or paste a warm one-paragraph intro if she wants one here too

Do this after Sections 1–4 are complete.

## Open questions to confirm with Jen

These are the decisions that need Jen's input before you finalize Section 4:

1. **Cancellation fee?** Default copy is fee-free and warm. Does she want a late-cancel / no-show fee added? If yes, what amount?
2. **Intake form validity period?** How many days should a completed intake form count as "current" before a returning client has to fill it again? Common defaults are 90, 180, or 365 days.
3. **Consent forms?** Which consents does she want included in the intake form flow? (Informed consent, telehealth consent, financial agreement, HIPAA notice, any others specific to her practice.)
4. **Booking window?** How far in advance can clients book, and how close to the appointment time can a booking still be made?

Once she answers these, this doc gets updated and Section 4 gets its final copy.
