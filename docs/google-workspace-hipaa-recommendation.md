# Google Workspace for HIPAA — Recommendation

**Prepared for:** Dr. Jennifer Wilson, PhD, LCSW
**Purpose:** Setting up a HIPAA-compliant email, calendar, and document workflow for your private practice.

---

## The Bottom Line

**Recommended plan: Google Workspace Business Standard — $14/user/month (~$168/year).**

This gives you a professional email address on your own domain (`hello@drjenniferwilson.com`), a signed HIPAA Business Associate Agreement (BAA) with Google, and the core tools you already use every day — Gmail, Calendar, Drive, Docs, and Meet — all covered for working with client information.

If you want the strongest compliance posture and extra peace of mind, step up to **Business Plus at $22/user/month (~$264/year)**. The added value there is Google Vault, which gives you retention, archiving, and audit trails an auditor would appreciate.

---

## What You Get

With a Business Standard or Business Plus plan and a signed BAA, the following Google services are covered for handling protected health information (PHI):

- **Gmail** on your custom domain — a professional, branded email address
- **Google Calendar** — scheduling and appointment management
- **Google Drive** with Docs, Sheets, Slides, and Forms — intake forms, clinical notes storage, secure document sharing
- **Google Meet** — video sessions (a reliable backup for telehealth)
- **Google Chat** — secure internal messaging if you add a team member later
- **Google Keep and Tasks** — notes and to-dos
- **Google Vault** (Business Plus only) — retention, eDiscovery, and compliance archiving
- **Gemini in Workspace** — AI assistance inside Gmail, Docs, and Drive, covered under the BAA

Your existing clinical platform (Jane App) remains your EHR and continues handling session notes, billing, and the clinical BAA for that side of your practice. Google Workspace covers the communications and documents side.

## Why Business Standard Is the Sweet Spot

For a solo practice, Business Standard gives you everything that matters:

- A professional `@yourdomain.com` email address that builds trust with clients and referral sources
- Generous 2 TB of storage — enough for years of intake forms, resources, and documents
- Google Meet with recording and noise cancellation for telehealth sessions
- Full HIPAA BAA coverage for the Google services you actually use
- Room to grow if you ever add an associate, assistant, or biller

Business Plus adds Vault, which is valuable if you want formal retention policies and audit-ready records, and worth the extra $8/month if compliance rigor is a priority for you.

## Steps to Set It Up

1. **Purchase a domain** if you don't already own one (e.g., `drjenniferwilson.com` through Google Domains or Namecheap, roughly $12/year).
2. **Sign up for Google Workspace Business Standard** at workspace.google.com and choose your domain during signup.
3. **Sign the HIPAA BAA** in the Admin console: Account → Legal & Compliance → Security and Privacy Additional Terms → Google Workspace/Cloud Identity HIPAA Business Associate Amendment → Review & Accept. It's a two-minute click-through.
4. **Configure for compliance:**
   - Enable two-factor authentication on your account
   - Disable any services not covered by the BAA (YouTube, Photos, third-party add-ons)
   - Set up strong password requirements
   - Keep work email and personal Gmail fully separated — different browsers or profiles help
5. **Document your setup** — keep a short written note of when you signed the BAA and what services you've enabled, as part of your HIPAA documentation file.

## A Few Things to Keep in Mind

- **You are still the covered entity.** Signing the BAA means Google takes responsibility for their side of the infrastructure. You remain responsible for how you use the tools, your workforce training (even if that workforce is just you), and your overall HIPAA risk assessment.
- **Stay inside the covered services.** When you use Gmail, Drive, Calendar, and Meet under your Workspace account, you're covered. Pasting client information into a personal Gmail, a ChatGPT window, or a third-party app outside the BAA breaks that coverage.
- **Jane App still handles clinical records.** This recommendation is for your administrative and communications backbone — email, scheduling, documents, and telehealth backup. Your clinical documentation stays in Jane App.
- **Prices and features can change.** The figures above reflect current published pricing and may shift. Confirm at checkout.
