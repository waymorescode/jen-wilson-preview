Benchmark Competitors

State of Mind KC — https://www.stateofmindkc.com

Overall Aesthetic: Warm, feminine, lifestyle-forward. Personal brand meets Instagram influencer energy. More lifestyle coach than clinical therapist.

Platform: Wix

Navigation:

  • Style: Sticky horizontal header with logo left, nav right
  • Items: 6 (Home, About, Tools, Investment, For Therapists, Book Now)
  • CTA: "BOOK NOW" as final nav item — styled as navigation link, not button
  • Mobile: Hamburger menu

Hero Section:

  • Type: Full-width text-only with stacked heading hierarchy. No hero image in the traditional sense — the hero IS the text.
  • Text alignment: Centered, large-scale display type
  • Headlines layered: small eyebrow ("Conversational. Encouraging. Compassionate.") then large display ("THERAPY FOR THE MODERN WOMAN") then sub ("WHO WANTS TO FEEL AS PUT TOGETHER AS SHE LOOKS")
  • CTA: None in hero itself — scroll-dependent
  • Animation: Text animation/fade-in on load

Homepage Section Order:

  1. Text-only hero with stacked headlines
  2. Split section: therapist photo left + specialization list right + "TELL ME MORE" CTA
  3. Featured-in media logos + therapist name banner
  4. Pain points section (vertical scrolling text list — "You work hard... You feel disconnected... Your perfectionism...")
  5. "It's time for something new" + 3-column services grid (1:1 Therapy, Online Therapy, Shop My Tools)
  6. Free download lead magnet section (The Overwhelm Cheat Sheet)
  7. About teaser with photo + bio paragraph ("Meet Greta!")
  8. Full-width quote banner ("Therapy has the power to change everything.")
  9. Split CTA section (Let's Work Together / Do Some Self-Help)
  10. Testimonial carousel (3 slides)
  11. Blog preview (2 posts)
  12. Footer with nav links, Instagram feed, social, free tools promotion

Grid & Layout:

  • Mix of full-width and contained. Sections alternate between single-column text and 2-column photo/text splits
  • Generous whitespace, but layout feels busy overall due to many sections
  • Some asymmetric photo/text pairing
  • Service cards use image-left/text-right pattern with stacked layout

Typography:

  • Headlines: Sans-serif, all-caps, dramatic scale (lifestyle/fashion aesthetic)
  • Body: Clean sans-serif
  • Heavy use of ALL CAPS for section headers and CTAs
  • Decorative: None — relies on scale and weight for hierarchy

Color Palette:

  • Primary: Off-white/cream backgrounds (~#FAF8F5)
  • Secondary: Warm pink/blush accents (from photography)
  • Text: Dark charcoal (~#333)
  • Buttons: Dark charcoal/black with white text
  • Overall: Light, warm, feminine

Image Style:

  • Professional lifestyle portraits — warm-toned, editorial quality
  • Therapist shown in fashionable casual clothing, modern settings
  • No clinical imagery whatsoever
  • Full-bleed photos, some with overlapping text blocks

CTA Strategy:

  • 8+ CTAs across homepage
  • Button styles: Text links in all-caps with underlines, occasional filled buttons
  • Language: "BOOK NOW," "TELL ME MORE," "LEARN MORE," "I'D LOVE THESE," "SEND IT TO MY INBOX"
  • No floating/sticky CTA

Footer:

  • Complex — nav links, Instagram feed grid (3 images), social handle, free tools promotion
  • Privacy/terms links

What Makes This Layout Distinctive:
The text-only hero with dramatically scaled, stacked typography is the signature move — it reads like a fashion editorial headline rather than a therapy site. The pain-points section uses scrolling text to create emotional resonance before presenting solutions. Overall, it positions therapy as a lifestyle upgrade rather than a clinical service.

Color Palette:

The asymmetric 24-column grid creates magazine-editorial visual rhythm — nothing is predictably centered or symmetrically stacked. The marquee animated text band adds energy and modernity. This site successfully positions a therapist as a media personality and thought leader, not just a clinician.

Layout Pattern Recommendations

Based on this research, here are 4 distinct layout approaches for Dr. Jennifer Wilson's boutique therapy practice in St. Augustine Beach, FL. Brand colors: sage green (#4A5D4A), cream (#F9F6F0), gold (#C4A265) with lotus logo.

1. "The Coastal Editorial"

Draws from: Minaa B (asymmetric grid), The Practice (marquee band), Mission Therapie (serif typography)

Hero: Split layout — professional portrait of Dr. Wilson on the left (60% width, full-height, warm-toned), headline and CTA on the right (40%) over cream background. Headline in elegant serif (Cormorant Garamond or similar): "Healing Starts with Feeling Safe." Gold accent on the CTA button. Subtle scroll-reveal animation.

Homepage Section Order:

  1. Split hero (photo + headline + dual CTAs)
  2. Scrolling marquee band listing specialties: TRAUMA RECOVERY | ANXIETY | EATING DISORDERS | COUPLES THERAPY | EMDR — in gold on sage green background
  3. Welcome text section (centered, generous whitespace)
  4. 3-column service cards (white on cream, sage green icons, gold "Learn more" links)
  5. About teaser (asymmetric — text left at 55%, circular photo right at 45%)
  6. Trust badges row (credentials, years of experience, court-qualified expert — icon + text)
  7. Testimonial section (large pull quote in serif, sage green left border)
  8. Full-width sage green CTA band ("Ready to Start?" + gold booking button)
  9. Footer

Grid/Column Strategy: 12-column asymmetric grid. Never perfectly centered — content blocks offset 1-2 columns for editorial rhythm. Alternating 60/40 and 40/60 splits. Generous 80-100px vertical spacing between sections.

Nav Style: Transparent on hero, white sticky with subtle shadow on scroll. Logo (lotus + "Dr. Jennifer Wilson") left, 4 nav items + gold "Book Consultation" pill button right.

Audience Signal: "Premium private practice with editorial sophistication — this therapist is an expert who takes her craft and her brand seriously." Signals competence and confidence without clinical coldness.

The editorial layout subconsciously signals authority (magazine = expert) while the warm photography and conversational copy maintain approachability. Perfect for the affluent St. Augustine woman who researches carefully before committing.

Key Takeaways Across All Research

  1. Every premium therapy site uses Squarespace. It's the clear platform winner for this market segment.
  2. Serif fonts signal premium — the most sophisticated sites (Mission Therapie, Wholeness Collective, The Practice) all use serif headings paired with sans-serif body.
  3. Asymmetric grids separate boutique from template — the premium sites deliberately offset content blocks rather than centering everything.
  4. Aggressive CTAs backfire in therapy — the most effective sites use conversational language ("Let's talk," "Learn more") rather than urgency ("Book NOW!").
  5. Full-width color bands create section rhythm without needing excessive imagery.
  6. Numbered/guided sections reduce decision anxiety — particularly effective for therapy sites where visitors are already stressed.
  7. The hero makes or breaks the first impression — split heroes and bold typography outperform generic stock photo overlays.
  8. Dark/moody works for luxury spa, NOT for therapy — Bathhouse's dark mode is aspirational reference but wrong for a therapist's trust-building needs.
