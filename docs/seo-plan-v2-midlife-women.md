# SEO Plan v2 — Therapy for Midlife Women (revised)

**For:** Jen &nbsp;·&nbsp; **From:** Rich &nbsp;·&nbsp; 2026-05-09 &nbsp;·&nbsp; Revised after SEO research fact-check &nbsp;·&nbsp; Supersedes `seo-page-edits.md`

> **Sources:** Every claim in this plan is grounded in the 2026 therapy-SEO research catalogued in `seo-research-sources.md` in this folder.

This plan combines two real findings from the research:

1. **Local + service intent drives bookings.** *"Therapist near me"* and *"anxiety counselor St. Augustine"* are searches by people who are ready to call. They convert. Multiple 2026 sources are emphatic that high-intent local searches outperform high-volume informational searches for actual client acquisition.
2. **Niche specialization works for boutique practices.** A therapist who clearly serves *one* well-defined audience (midlife women, perimenopause, betrayal recovery) is more findable and more bookable than a generalist. There are working examples in the research.

The first finding is the foundation. The second finding tells us *who* to specialize for. Together they yield the architecture below.

---

## What changed from the first draft

The first draft of this plan led with emotional, conversational keywords (*"I feel invisible in my marriage,"* *"I don't feel like myself anymore"*) as primary keyword targets. The 2026 SEO research shows that approach has the priority backwards:

- Emotional informational searches bring **traffic** but few **inquiries** — those searchers are top-of-funnel, often not ready to book a $200/session private-pay therapist.
- Local + service-intent searches bring **fewer visitors** but **far more bookings** — those searchers know what they need and want to hire someone now.

So the structure is now:

| Priority | Lane | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **#1** | Google Business Profile + local schema + NAP consistency | "Your GBP is often more important than your website for local SEO." Highest-intent searches happen here, in the map pack. |
| **#2** | Existing-page on-page edits (titles, metas, H1s with service + location keywords) | Low effort, high return. Captures clients searching for what you do, where you are. |
| **#3** | Six pillar pages — primary keyword = service + location, supporting H2s = emotional language | Niche specialization compounds. Service + location wins the booking; emotional H2s win voice search, AI search, and featured snippets. |
| **#4** | Specialty pages (adoption home study, supervision, forensic) | Different audience, different referral channels. |
| **#5** | Content (quarterly blog) | Slow burn. Supports the pillars; doesn't replace them. |

## The page architecture

> **Page template (applied to every pillar):**
> - **Page title and H1** = service + location keyword (this is what Google ranks)
> - **Warm opening** (3–4 sentences, voice-checked) names the audience and the feeling
> - **6–8 H2 sections** — each one phrased as a real search question (*"I can't shut my brain off — what high-functioning anxiety really is"*). These ARE supporting content, not primary signals. They earn voice search, ChatGPT/Perplexity citations, and Google's "People Also Ask" box.
> - **Each H2** answered with 2–3 paragraphs of your real clinical perspective
> - **"How I work with this"** section that names your modalities (psychodynamic, CBT, breathwork) without jargon
> - **CTA** to the matching intensive + book a consultation

This structure is research-backed: service + location for the booking-intent searcher, conversational H2s for the AI/voice/snippet surface area.

| Phrase | Why we cut it |
|---|---|
| *Luxury therapy practice* | Off-brand. Your voice is warm and grounded, not luxury. "Boutique" is fine; "luxury" reads as commodity. |

| *"Why do I feel crazy in perimenopause?"* | We use it as an SEO target only — but the page never validates the word *crazy.* The H2 reframes it: *"Why am I so emotional in my 40s and 50s?"* Same searcher, kinder landing. |
