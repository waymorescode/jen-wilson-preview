## What your marketing folks were pointing at

Your marketing people were right. The site looks and reads beautifully, and the words on the page are in your voice — that part is solid. The piece that was missing is everything *underneath* the words: the signals search engines (Google, Bing) and AI assistants (ChatGPT, Claude) use to understand who you are, where you work, and what you do. Without those signals, the site could exist for years and still stay invisible to the exact people looking for a therapist in St. Augustine Beach.

The good news is this is fixable, and the fix has three layers: things I can do on the site, things we register with Google and the directories, and things that build over time as your practice shows up in the world.

---

## What I just did (Phase 1 — technical foundations)

These are the things search engines look for first. Without them, a site doesn't rank — period.

1. **Crawl map.** Added `robots.txt` and `sitemap.xml` so Google knows every page exists and has permission to index them. Without these, Google has to guess at your site structure.

2. **Page titles rewritten for search.** Every page now opens with the phrases people actually type — things like *"Therapist in St. Augustine Beach"* and *"Dr. Jennifer Wilson, PhD, LCSW."* The old titles led with the brand name *Conscious Practice*, which nobody searches for yet. Now the titles match the search.

3. **Structured data (the hidden labels).** Added schema.org JSON-LD markup to every page so search engines understand:
    - *This is a medical/therapy practice, not a blog.*
    - *Dr. Jennifer Wilson is a PhD, LCSW with 20+ years of experience.*
    - *The practice serves St. Augustine Beach, Jacksonville, and all of Florida.*
    - *These are the five intensives, priced at $1,800 and $3,200.*
   Google uses this to show rich results (stars, prices, hours) instead of plain blue links. AI assistants use it to answer "who's a good therapist in St. Augustine Beach?" accurately.

4. **Social sharing cards.** Added Open Graph and Twitter Card tags. When somebody shares your site in a text, email, Facebook, LinkedIn, or Slack, it now shows a proper preview with the golden-hour beach image, your name, and a clean description — instead of a bare URL.

5. **Canonical URLs.** Every page now declares its own official address. This prevents duplicate-content penalties and keeps all the search credit flowing to the right URL.

6. **Favicon swap.** Replaced the generic leaf emoji with the site's lotus logo so the tiny icon in browser tabs matches the brand.

## What's next (Phase 2 — I'll do these with you)

7. **Google Search Console.** This is Google's dashboard for site owners. Free. We submit the site, verify ownership, submit the sitemap, and from that point on Google starts telling us which search terms are bringing people in, which pages are ranking, and where the gaps are.

8. **Bing Webmaster Tools.** Same idea, for Bing and — importantly — for ChatGPT/Copilot, which pull from Bing's index. Ten-minute setup, big downstream effect.

9. **Google Analytics / lightweight analytics.** Light privacy-respecting tracking so we can see traffic without creeping on visitors. You already own Google Workspace Business Plus, so the account is in place.

## What you'll do (Phase 3 — the off-site backlinks)

This part is you, because Google treats listings *you* create as trust signals that *Rich* can't manufacture.

10. **Google Business Profile.** This is the big one. Claim the map pin at `google.com/business`. Add hours, phone, the golden beach photo, the lotus logo, services, and a short description. When somebody searches *"therapist St. Augustine Beach"* and Google shows the three-pack map with photos — **that's this.** Without it, you're not in that map pack. With it, you are.

11. **Psychology Today directory.** The industry standard. Around $30/month. Practically every therapist in the country is on it; it's where people search when they're looking in their insurance network or for a specific specialty. It also gives you a strong backlink from a high-authority site, which helps everything else rank.

12. **Free directories worth claiming:**
    - GoodTherapy.org — free listing, another credibility backlink.
    - TherapyDen — free, newer, growing.
    - NPI Registry — free, already exists (everyone with an NPI is listed), worth verifying your info is accurate.
    - Your professional associations (NASW Florida Chapter, any institutes you're affiliated with) — free backlinks.

I'll put together a one-page checklist with direct links and exact copy to paste when you're ready to sit down with these.

## What happens over time (Phase 4 — the slow-burn win)

13. **Content.** Short monthly pieces in your voice on topics clients actually search: *"What to expect from a therapy intensive," "How to tell if you need a relationship edit," "Empty nest and the question underneath it."* Two to three pieces a quarter is plenty. Each one becomes a new door into the site.

14. **Reviews.** After someone finishes an intensive or completes a meaningful stretch of work, a gentle nudge toward a Google review (on your Business Profile) and a Psychology Today review. Both feed directly back into search ranking and both signal to the next person that this practice is real and working.
