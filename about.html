About Dr. Jennifer Wilson

Trauma Therapist | St. Augustine, FL

I Believe in the Power of Healing

I became a therapist because I believe everyone deserves a safe space to heal and grow—especially women who've been told to "just push through" for too long.

After witnessing the transformative power of therapy in my own life and the lives of those around me, I knew this was my calling. The moment I saw my first client recognize their own strength after months of feeling broken, I knew I was exactly where I belonged.

Today, after 20 years and thousands of clinical hours, what still drives me is that moment of recognition—when someone realizes they're not broken, they're healing. That spark of hope, that first genuine smile after months of pain, that's why I show up every day.

My Approach to Therapy

I believe healing happens in relationship—in being truly seen, heard, and understood without judgment. My approach combines evidence-based methods like EMDR and CBT with genuine human connection and compassion.

I don't believe in one-size-fits-all therapy. Together, we'll find what works for YOU—whether that's processing trauma through EMDR, challenging anxiety patterns with CBT, or finding peace through mindfulness practices. You're the expert on your life; I'm here to walk beside you with tools, support, and deep respect for your pace and process.

What I Specialize In

Trauma & PTSD Recovery

With 20+ years focused on trauma, I help clients process and heal from:

  • Childhood trauma and abuse
  • Sexual trauma and assault
  • Medical trauma
  • Accidents and natural disasters
  • Complex PTSD

I'm trained in EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing), which helps your brain reprocess traumatic memories so they lose their emotional charge.

Anxiety & Depression in High-Achieving Women

I understand the unique pressures of trying to do it all perfectly. I help with:

  • Perfectionism and imposter syndrome
  • Overwhelming anxiety and panic attacks
  • Burnout and exhaustion
  • Work-life balance
  • Postpartum anxiety and depression

Eating Disorders & Body Image

Recovery is possible. I provide compassionate support for:

  • Anorexia, bulimia, and binge eating disorder
  • Emotional eating and food obsession
  • Body dysmorphia
  • Diet culture recovery
  • Building a peaceful relationship with food and your body

A Little About My Life

I live in St. Augustine Beach with my family, where my husband and I are raising our wonderful (and sometimes muddy!) little boy. The natural beauty and healing quality of the coast drew us here—the same peaceful, restorative elements I try to bring into my therapy practice.

When I'm not in my office, you'll find me walking on the beach with my family, exploring St. Augustine's historic district, or curled up with a good book. As a mother myself, I understand the unique challenges of trying to be everything to everyone while your own needs go unmet.

I believe deeply in practicing what I preach about self-care. For me, that means beach walks, family time, good coffee, and remembering that we're all doing the best we can with what we have.

Supporting the Professional Community

In addition to my therapy practice, I provide clinical supervision for therapists pursuing licensure and consultation to nonprofit organizations serving vulnerable populations. This work allows me to support the broader mental health community while maintaining my primary focus on individual and family therapy.
