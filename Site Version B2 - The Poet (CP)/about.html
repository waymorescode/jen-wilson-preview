About Jennifer

PhD, LCSW | St. Augustine Beach

Some people find their way to this work by accident. I found it by listening.

For nearly twenty years, I've sat with people at the edges of their own stories—the ones they tell themselves, the ones they tell others, the ones they're finally ready to tell someone who won't flinch. I've worked with children who experienced unthinkable things. With families fracturing under invisible pressure. With individuals so skilled at managing that they've forgotten what they actually want.

I taught at universities. Directed clinical services. Trained other clinicians. Wrote about trauma and its ripples. The research mattered. But what stayed with me were the faces—the moment someone realizes they don't have to keep holding their breath.

How I Work

Therapy in my office is slower than it sounds. Deeper. I weave in what actually helps: presence, genuine curiosity, the kind of attention that makes someone feel genuinely seen. I work psychoanalytically, which means we're not just managing symptoms—we're understanding the architecture of your patterns. I bring mindfulness in. Self-care. The nervous system as wisdom, not enemy.

I'm trained to challenge you in ways that land. To notice what you might miss. To sit with discomfort without rushing past it. To believe you're more capable than you think.

Credentials & Depth

I'm a Licensed Clinical Social Worker (Florida #8431), qualified supervisor, research-published. I've completed doctoral study focused on how early trauma shapes development. I trained at Cornell. But what those letters mean is: I've done the deep work. I've spent thousands of hours in rooms like this one. I know my limitations and my strengths. I'll never pretend to know you better than you know yourself.

Why Florida, Why Here

My husband and I chose St. Augustine Beach because it felt like home before we moved here. My son goes to school with your kids. I shop at the same markets. I'm embedded in this community—not hovering above it. You'll see me around. That matters to me. It means I'm doing this work with skin in the game.
