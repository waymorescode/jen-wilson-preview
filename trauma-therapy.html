Trauma & PTSD Recovery

Specialized EMDR Therapy for Healing from Trauma

You Don't Have to Carry This Alone

Trauma isn't just what happened to you—it's what's still happening inside you. The nightmares, the hypervigilance, the way certain sounds or smells send you right back to that moment. The exhaustion of always being on guard. The shame you carry even though none of it was your fault.

After 20 years specializing in trauma treatment and over 5,000 clinical hours helping people heal from PTSD, I can tell you this with absolute certainty: healing is possible. You're not broken. Your brain is doing exactly what it's supposed to do after trauma—and we can help it learn to feel safe again.

Types of Trauma I Specialize In

Childhood Trauma & Abuse

Growing up in an unsafe environment leaves lasting marks. Whether it was physical abuse, emotional neglect, witnessing domestic violence, or growing up with addiction in your home—these experiences shape how you see yourself and the world. We'll work together to process these memories and help you reclaim the childhood you deserved.

Sexual Trauma & Assault

Sexual trauma carries unique layers of shame, self-blame, and violation. Whether it happened recently or decades ago, whether it was a stranger or someone you trusted, your pain is valid and healing is possible. I provide a safe, non-judgmental space to process what happened at your own pace.

Complex PTSD

When trauma wasn't a single event but ongoing—repeated abuse, chronic neglect, long-term domestic violence—it creates complex PTSD. This affects your sense of self, your relationships, and your ability to regulate emotions. Treatment for complex PTSD requires specialized care, which I provide through trauma-informed therapy and EMDR.

Medical Trauma

Traumatic medical experiences—difficult childbirth, life-threatening illness, invasive procedures, chronic pain—can leave you feeling unsafe in your own body. I help clients process medical trauma and rebuild trust in their bodies and healthcare.

Accidents & Natural Disasters

Car accidents, natural disasters, sudden loss—these single-incident traumas can create lasting PTSD symptoms. EMDR is particularly effective for these types of trauma, often bringing significant relief in 3-6 months.

What is EMDR Therapy?

EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing) is an evidence-based therapy specifically designed to treat trauma and PTSD. It's recognized by the American Psychiatric Association, the World Health Organization, and the Department of Defense as an effective treatment for trauma.

How EMDR Works

When you experience trauma, your brain can't process the memory normally. It gets "stuck" in your nervous system with all the emotions, physical sensations, and beliefs from that moment. That's why trauma memories feel so present—your brain hasn't filed them away as "past."

EMDR uses bilateral stimulation (eye movements, tapping, or sounds) to help your brain reprocess these stuck memories. It's like helping your brain finish the job it started—processing the trauma so it becomes a memory you have, not a memory that has you.

What to Expect

EMDR doesn't require you to talk in detail about what happened (though you can if you want to). You don't have to relive the trauma. Instead, we work with your brain's natural healing process to reduce the emotional charge of traumatic memories.

Many clients notice significant improvement within 3-6 months, though complex trauma may take longer. The goal isn't to forget what happened—it's to remember it without the overwhelming emotions and physical reactions.

Do You Have PTSD?

PTSD looks different for everyone, but common signs include:

  • Intrusive memories, flashbacks, or nightmares
  • Avoiding people, places, or things that remind you of the trauma
  • Feeling constantly on edge or easily startled
  • Difficulty sleeping or concentrating
  • Emotional numbness or feeling disconnected
  • Intense anxiety or panic attacks
  • Difficulty trusting others or feeling safe
  • Shame, guilt, or feeling like it was your fault
  • Anger or irritability that seems out of proportion
  • Physical symptoms like headaches, stomach issues, or chronic pain

If several of these resonate with you, trauma therapy could help.

What Trauma Therapy Looks Like

Phase 1: Building Safety & Stability (2-4 sessions)

Before we process trauma, we make sure you have the tools to feel safe. We'll work on grounding techniques, coping skills, and building resources. You're in control of the pace.

Phase 2: Processing Trauma (Ongoing)

Using EMDR and other trauma-focused techniques, we'll work through traumatic memories. This isn't about reliving the trauma—it's about helping your brain process it so it loses its emotional charge.

Phase 3: Integration & Moving Forward

As trauma symptoms decrease, we focus on rebuilding your life. This might include improving relationships, addressing depression or anxiety, and creating the life you want—not just surviving, but thriving.

You've Survived the Trauma. Now Let's Help You Heal.

You don't have to live with PTSD symptoms forever. Healing is possible, and you don't have to do it alone.
