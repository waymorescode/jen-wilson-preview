Anxiety & Depression Treatment

Find Relief from Overwhelm, Perfectionism, and Burnout

When "Just Push Through" Stops Working

You've been pushing through for so long. Waking up at 2 AM with your mind racing through tomorrow's to-do list. Smiling through the day while feeling hollow inside. Telling yourself you should be grateful, you have so much—so why does everything feel so hard?

The panic attacks that come out of nowhere. The exhaustion that sleep doesn't fix. The constant worry that you're not doing enough, being enough. The way getting out of bed feels like climbing a mountain some days.

You're not weak. You're not failing. You're experiencing anxiety and depression—and there's a way through this that doesn't involve just "trying harder."

I Specialize in Treating

Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

The constant worry that follows you everywhere. The "what-ifs" that never stop. The tension in your shoulders that's become so normal you barely notice it anymore. GAD makes you feel like you're waiting for the other shoe to drop—always. We'll work together to quiet that anxious voice and help you feel safe in your own life.

Panic Attacks & Panic Disorder

The sudden terror, racing heart, feeling like you can't breathe or you're dying. Then the fear of having another panic attack, which makes you avoid places and situations. Panic attacks are terrifying, but they're also treatable. I'll teach you tools to manage panic in the moment and address the underlying anxiety driving them.

Social Anxiety

The fear of being judged, saying the wrong thing, or being the center of attention. Replaying conversations for hours afterward, convinced you embarrassed yourself. Social anxiety can be isolating, but you don't have to live with it. We'll work on building confidence and quieting that critical inner voice.

Perfectionism & Imposter Syndrome

You've achieved so much, but you still feel like a fraud. Like any moment someone will discover you don't actually know what you're doing. You set impossibly high standards and beat yourself up when you can't meet them. Perfectionism isn't about excellence—it's about fear. Let's address what's really driving it.

Depression & Hopelessness

When everything feels gray and pointless. When you're going through the motions but not really living. When you can't remember the last time you felt genuinely happy. Depression lies to you—it tells you this is forever, that nothing will help. But depression is treatable, and you can feel like yourself again.

Postpartum Anxiety & Depression

You thought you'd feel joy, but instead you feel terrified, overwhelmed, or numb. The intrusive thoughts about something bad happening to your baby. The guilt that you're not enjoying motherhood like you "should." Postpartum anxiety and depression are common, treatable, and not your fault. You deserve support.

Burnout & Overwhelm

You're exhausted in a way that sleep doesn't fix. You've been running on empty for so long you can't remember what "full" feels like. Work, kids, household, relationships—it's all too much, but you don't know how to stop. Burnout is a sign you need support, not a sign you're weak.

How I Help You Heal

I use evidence-based approaches tailored to your specific needs. This isn't one-size-fits-all therapy—we'll find what works for YOU.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

CBT helps you identify and change the thought patterns that fuel anxiety and depression. Those automatic thoughts that tell you you're not good enough, that something terrible will happen, that there's no point in trying—we'll challenge them together and build new, healthier patterns.

Mindfulness & Acceptance-Based Approaches

Sometimes the problem isn't the anxiety itself—it's the anxiety about the anxiety. Mindfulness helps you change your relationship with difficult emotions instead of fighting them. You'll learn to observe thoughts and feelings without being controlled by them.

Addressing Root Causes

Anxiety and depression often have roots in past experiences, trauma, or learned patterns. We won't just manage symptoms—we'll address what's underneath. This might include processing past trauma, healing attachment wounds, or changing relationship patterns.

Practical Tools You Can Use Today

I'll teach you concrete strategies for managing anxiety and depression in your daily life: grounding techniques for panic attacks, thought-challenging exercises, sleep hygiene, self-compassion practices, and boundary-setting skills. You'll leave each session with tools you can actually use.

When to Seek Help

You don't have to wait until you're in crisis. Consider therapy if:

  • Anxiety or worry interferes with your daily life, work, or relationships
  • You're having panic attacks or avoiding places/situations due to anxiety
  • You feel sad, empty, or hopeless most days
  • You've lost interest in things you used to enjoy
  • You're struggling to sleep, or sleeping too much
  • You feel exhausted even after rest
  • You're irritable, on edge, or snapping at loved ones
  • You're using alcohol, food, or other substances to cope
  • You feel like you're just going through the motions
  • You're having thoughts of self-harm or suicide

If you're having thoughts of suicide, please call 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) or go to your nearest emergency room. You matter, and help is available.

What to Expect in Therapy

First Session: Assessment & Goal Setting

We'll talk about what brought you to therapy, what you're experiencing, and what you want to change. I'll ask questions to understand your history and current symptoms. Together, we'll set goals for our work.

Early Sessions: Building Skills & Understanding Patterns

We'll start with tools you can use immediately—grounding techniques, coping strategies, thought-challenging exercises. We'll also explore the patterns and triggers that fuel your anxiety or depression.

Ongoing Work: Deeper Healing & Lasting Change

As symptoms improve, we'll address root causes—past trauma, relationship patterns, core beliefs about yourself. The goal isn't just to feel better temporarily, but to create lasting change.

Timeline

Many clients notice improvement within 8-12 sessions, though everyone's timeline is different. Some people need short-term support (3-6 months), while others benefit from longer-term therapy. We'll check in regularly about your progress and adjust as needed.

You Don't Have to Feel This Way Forever

Anxiety and depression are treatable. You can feel like yourself again—maybe even better than before.
