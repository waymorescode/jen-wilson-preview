About Jennifer

PhD, LCSW | St. Augustine Beach

Her Story

I grew up watching my own family navigate difficult things — the kind of struggles that don't always show up on the surface. I saw how much difference it made when someone actually listened, when someone had the patience and the skill to help people understand what was really happening beneath the surface. That shaped everything about how I work now.

I studied psychology and social work because I wanted to understand development and how early experiences shape us. My graduate work and my dissertation focused on the long-term impact of childhood trauma — not because I wanted to specialize in it, but because I wanted to understand how resilience actually works, and what actually helps people heal. That research led me into clinical practice, and clinical practice has been the most important education I could have gotten.

The Work Over Time

I spent years as director of clinical services at a nonprofit in Palm Beach, training therapists, developing programs, and working with young people who'd experienced significant harm. I taught at multiple universities — graduate seminars, doctoral-level courses — which pushed me to stay sharp and to think about what actually works, not just what sounds good.

But over time, I realized that my deepest work happens in private practice, one person or couple at a time. That's where real change happens. That's where I can show up fully.

How I Work

I'm trained in psychoanalytic therapy — the kind of deep, exploratory work that doesn't rush. I weave in mindfulness and somatic awareness because stress and emotion live in the body, not just the mind. I'm trained to see patterns in how people developed, how their families worked, why they respond the way they do now. But I also know that understanding isn't enough — change happens through experience, through being met differently, through trying something new in the presence of someone who understands what you're working with.

I don't see therapy as fixing something broken. I see it as investing in something better — clarity, resilience, deeper relationships, the freedom to be yourself without that constant undercurrent of doubt or anxiety.

My Credentials (The Important Parts)

Licensed Clinical Social Worker in Florida (#8431) since 2006. PhD in social welfare with a research focus on developmental psychology and trauma. Qualified Supervisor by the Florida Board of Clinical Social Work. Cornell University Summer Research Institute alumna. Published researcher with peer-reviewed journal articles and book chapters.

But here's what those mean: they represent discipline, ongoing education, and a commitment to staying sharp. They mean I've been through rigorous training and I understand the science. But the real credential is that people's lives have gotten better because of our work together.

Who I Am

I live here in St. Augustine Beach with my husband and son. My kids go to school here. I shop at the same stores you do. I'm woven into this community, not floating above it. That matters to me. When I work with you, I'm not a faceless name on a clinical website. I'm a neighbor, a fellow parent, someone who gets what it's like to live in this place.

I'm also genuinely interested in people. Not in a clinical way — not dissecting you as a case study. But in a real way. Your life matters to me. The things you're working with matter. And I show up with that in mind.
