Focused Intensives for Specific Life Moments

You've experienced a recent break-up, a life-changing event, or a realization that you've lost yourself. Perhaps you're a new mom, maybe you're experiencing the changes of perimenopause, or your children have just left for college. These intensive therapy packages are the jumpstart you need.

They're for people who know what they want to work on and are ready to fully commit — to show up, do the work, and experience real transformation.

Each intensive is 6 or 12 weeks with structured sessions (usually twice a week to start), plus between-session support and touchpoints. They're high-touch and limited enrollment.

What's Included in Every Intensive

Intensive Sessions

Weekly or twice-weekly sessions, depending on the intensive. These aren't rushed—they're designed for depth and momentum.

Personalized Approach

We build a roadmap together. The intensive is shaped around your specific goals and timeline — fully personalized to your journey.

Support Between Sessions

Email access for pressing matters between sessions. This matters — the healing continues throughout your daily life, extending well beyond each session.

Tools and Practices

You learn practical, evidence-based tools—mindfulness, somatic practices, communication skills—that you can use long after the intensive ends.

Integration Sessions

The final 1-2 sessions focus on consolidating what you've learned and planning how to sustain this work after the intensive concludes.

Limited Enrollment

I keep my caseload small and accept a limited number of intensive clients at a time. This ensures you get my best, most focused attention.

Why Limited Enrollment?

I keep my practice intentionally small. When we do these intensives, I'm committing to a small number of people for that block of time. That means I'm fully present and deeply focused. You feel that warmth and attention. The work is better because of it. Your wellness is worth it.

A portion of every intensive supports a Woman-In-Need session — because every woman deserves access to compassionate care.
