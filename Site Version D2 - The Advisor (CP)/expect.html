What to Expect

How our work together unfolds

Starting Out

When you reach out, you'll schedule a consultation call. This isn't an intake session or a sales pitch. It's a chance for us to talk about what's happening in your life and what you're hoping might shift. I'll ask questions. I'll listen carefully. And I'll be honest about whether I'm the right fit for what you need.

Most consultations are 20 minutes. You'll get a sense of how I listen, and I'll get a sense of what we'd be working with. From there, we decide together if we want to move forward.

The First Session

If we both agree to work together, your first full session is typically 60 minutes. We'll go deeper into your history, your relationships, what brought you here. I'll ask about your family, how you were raised, patterns you've noticed in yourself. This isn't random — I'm building a picture of how you developed and what makes sense about how you respond to stress or conflict or the particular challenge you're facing.

You might feel seen in new ways. You might also feel tired. That's normal. You're doing real work.

How Ongoing Therapy Works

Sessions are typically 50 minutes, once a week. Some people do twice a week, especially early on or when something major is shifting. We work at the pace that matches what you need.

What happens in those sessions is a mix. We talk about what's happening now. We explore patterns that go back further — family dynamics, early experiences, ways of being that made sense once but might not anymore. We sit with difficult emotions. We notice things. Sometimes I challenge you gently. Sometimes I reflect back what I'm hearing. Sometimes we're quiet together.

The work is collaborative. I'm not the expert on your life — you are. I'm the expert on how to help you become clearer about your own life, and how to make different choices from that clarity.

What's Different About My Approach

I don't work from a protocol or a manual. I'm trained in psychoanalytic therapy, which means I'm attuned to your interior world — not just what you say, but how you say it, what you avoid, what lights up when you talk. I listen for patterns.

I also work somatically — meaning I understand that what you feel in your body, how you hold yourself, how you breathe under stress — all of that is information. Change happens in the body too, not just in the mind.

And I bring mindfulness into our work. Not as a separate practice, but woven through — the ability to notice your own experience without judgment, to stay present to what's actually happening instead of running a story about it.

Between Sessions

For most people, work happens in the session and then it continues in their life. They notice things differently. They make different choices. They sit with something that came up.

If you're in one of our boutique wellness packages, there's more structured support between sessions — check-ins, sometimes a short practice or reflection you're working with. But for regular therapy, the work is what you do in your daily life with new awareness.

The Pace

Therapy isn't linear. There are weeks where something major shifts and you feel different. There are weeks where you feel like you're going in circles. Both are part of it. I'm here to help you make sense of both.

Some people work with me for a few focused months around a specific transition. Some people work with me for years, deepening their self-understanding and shifting long-held patterns. There's no prescription. We look at what's actually helping and adjust from there.
