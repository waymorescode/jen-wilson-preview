What to Expect

How our work together unfolds

The Consultation

This is our first conversation—20-30 minutes to explore what's going on and whether we're the right fit for each other.

I'll ask about what brought you here, what you're hoping to get out of therapy, and what matters most to you. You'll get a sense of how I work and what the process looks like. There are no intake forms, no pressure, and no obligation to move forward. It's simply a chance to see if we align.

By the end of the call, you'll know whether you want to take the next step.

Your First Session

If we move forward, your first full session happens within a week or two of your consultation.

We'll spend time getting a fuller picture of your story—where you've been, what you're working with, what brought you to this moment. This isn't an interrogation. It's a real conversation that helps me understand you and what you're navigating.

Together, we'll begin building a sense of direction. Not a rigid plan, but a shared understanding of what success looks like for you. We'll talk about how often we'll meet, what our pace will be, and how we'll know when we're moving in the right direction.

How Sessions Work

Individual Sessions: 50 minutes, typically weekly or biweekly. We meet consistently so you have a dependable space to explore, process, and practice new ways of being.

Couples Sessions: 75 minutes, typically weekly. We work together on deepening connection, navigating conflict with skill, and rebuilding understanding.

Between Sessions: You have my email for urgent matters. Clients often jot down reflections or questions between sessions—this matters. Therapy doesn't just happen in the room; it happens in the space between sessions when you're living your life and noticing what's shifted.

Scheduling: We work together to find a time that works. Session slots are reserved for you, so consistency is important. If life happens and you need to reschedule, I ask for 48 hours' notice.

The Approach

My work is rooted in psychoanalytic depth—we go beyond symptoms to understand patterns, stories, and what's underneath the surface. I blend this with mindfulness-informed practice and evidence-based techniques.

This means:

  • We slow down. Real change takes time. We don't rush.
  • We explore. Why do you respond the way you do? What serves you, and what holds you back?
  • We practice. We talk about insight, but we also practice new ways of being in session so you can take them into your life.
  • We stay honest. If something isn't working, we adjust. If I notice a pattern you might not see, I'll gently point it out.
  • We respect your pace. Some clients are ready to dive deep. Others need to build trust first. Both are right.

What Changes Look Like

Change isn't always dramatic. Sometimes it's subtle—a different response to something that used to trigger you. A conversation you finally had. A boundary you set. A moment of self-compassion that wouldn't have been possible before.

For some, it's deeper: a fundamental shift in how you see yourself, your relationships, or what's possible. For others, it's about having support through a specific season—a life transition, a relationship challenge, a question about who you want to be.

We'll check in regularly about what's shifting. These observations matter. They help us know we're on the right track.
