What to Expect

How our work together unfolds

The Consultation Call

30 minutes. No cost. No obligation.

We'll talk about what's going on—what brought you to therapy, what you're hoping for, and whether we're the right fit. I'm listening not just to what you're dealing with, but to how you think about your life and what matters most to you.

No intake forms on the call. No pressure. Just real conversation and clarity. You'll know, and I'll know, whether this is something we want to move forward with together.

Your First Session

50 minutes. Listening for patterns.

We'll go deeper. I'm listening not just for the presenting problem, but for the patterns underneath. How did you learn to handle difficulty? What parts of yourself have you had to hide or minimize? What would it feel like to live differently?

We won't solve everything in one session. But you'll leave knowing you've been heard and that we have direction.

How Sessions Work

Frequency

Weekly or biweekly—whatever pace serves you best. Some people do intense weekly work early on, then move to every other week as things stabilize. We adjust based on your life and your needs.

Duration

50 minutes per session. That's a full clinical hour—time for real work without rushing, time for pauses and reflection, time to genuinely explore what's happening.

Location

My office in St. Augustine Beach. Or telehealth if that works better for your schedule or life situation. Both are fully supported.

Consistency

Same time, same day. Consistency creates safety and momentum. Your brain starts preparing for the work, and we build real continuity between sessions.

The Approach

I'm not telling you what to do

This isn't advice-giving. I'm helping you think differently, access your own wisdom, and understand what's been driving your choices. Real change happens when you become curious about your own experience, not when someone tells you what's right.

Nervous system work

Therapy isn't only about thinking differently. It's about your body knowing it's safe. Through mindfulness, somatic awareness, and relational presence, we help your nervous system settle so that new learning is possible.

Depth over speed

We're not checking boxes or rushing toward "resolution." We're doing deep, careful work that actually changes how you relate to yourself and your life. That takes time. And it's worth it.

What Changes Look Like

Not linear. Some weeks you'll feel significantly different. Other weeks it'll feel like nothing's changing. But that's not how therapy works. Change accumulates. You'll look back six months from now and realize how much has shifted.

Quiet change. Therapy often isn't dramatic. It's subtle. You notice you're not spiraling the same way. You handle conflict differently. You sleep better. You're less reactive. You feel more yourself. That's real change.

Visible in 6 months. Most people start noticing meaningful shifts around the 6-month mark. You've done enough work that new patterns have started settling in. You're different—calmer, clearer, more resourced. Your relationships reflect it.
