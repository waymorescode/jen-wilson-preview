Client Policies & Agreements

Professional Relationship

The therapeutic relationship is a professional relationship. While I care about your wellbeing and approach this work with genuine investment, the relationship exists within professional boundaries. I am not your friend, but your therapist. This distinction exists to protect the integrity and effectiveness of our work together.

Appointments & Scheduling

Sessions are 50 minutes in length. Appointments are scheduled at mutually agreed times. Consistency is important—same day, same time each week creates safety and momentum. Sessions may be in-person at my office in St. Augustine Beach or via secure telehealth.

If you arrive more than 15 minutes late, we may need to reschedule to maintain the integrity of the session and respect for the next client. You will be charged the full session fee if you arrive late without prior notice.

Cancellation Policy

I require 48 hours notice for cancellations. If you cancel with fewer than 48 hours notice, you will be charged the full session fee. This policy is in place to maintain my schedule and to commit to clients who are in the waiting room.

If you are unable to attend a scheduled session without notice, you will be charged the full session fee. If this happens repeatedly, we may need to pause your therapy and reassess your readiness to commit to the work.

Fees & Payment

Individual therapy is $200 per 50-minute session. Couples therapy is $300 per 50-minute session. Payment is due at the time of service. We can arrange payment via check, transfer, or credit card.

I practice on a private pay basis. I provide superbills after each session for clients who wish to submit to their insurance for out-of-network reimbursement. Please check with your insurance regarding coverage and out-of-network benefits.

If your account becomes delinquent, I may need to suspend services until payment is received. We can discuss payment arrangements or alternative options if cost is a barrier.

Confidentiality

Everything shared in therapy is confidential. This confidentiality is protected by law. I will not share information about your therapy without your written consent, except in circumstances required or permitted by law.

The legal exceptions to confidentiality include: (1) if I believe you are at immediate risk of harming yourself, (2) if you present a clear danger to others, (3) if there is abuse of a child or vulnerable adult, (4) if required by a court order, or (5) if you are in therapy as a condition of the court.

Communication Between Sessions

I respond to emails for administrative purposes (scheduling, payment, forms) and clinical questions within 24-48 hours during business days. Email is not appropriate for crisis situations.

If you are experiencing a mental health crisis or suicidal thoughts, please call 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) immediately. If you are in immediate danger, call 911 or go to your nearest emergency room.

Telehealth Sessions

Telehealth sessions are conducted via a HIPAA-compliant secure platform. By participating in telehealth, you agree to meet in a private, secure location where your session cannot be overheard or observed by others.

Florida law requires that I be able to verify your location during telehealth sessions. Telehealth is only available to clients physically located in Florida.

Technical difficulties may occasionally occur. If the connection is lost, I will attempt to reach you by phone. If we cannot reconnect within a few minutes, we will reschedule the session with no charge.

Termination of Therapy

Either of us can terminate therapy at any time. If you wish to end therapy, we will discuss this in session and ensure a thoughtful closure to our work together. I may also terminate therapy if I believe it is no longer beneficial, if you are unable to pay your fees, or if the therapeutic relationship is not serving your needs.

If I terminate therapy, I will provide referrals to other clinicians who may be a better fit for your needs.

Records

I maintain clinical records of our sessions. Florida law requires that I keep these records for a minimum of 7 years. You have the right to request access to your records and to receive copies.

There may be a copying and administrative fee for providing records. If you are concerned about information in your file, we can discuss this in session.

Social Media

I maintain professional boundaries with clients and do not connect with current or former clients on personal social media accounts. This boundary exists to protect your privacy and the integrity of our therapeutic relationship.

Limitations of Therapy

While therapy can be powerful and transformative, it cannot guarantee specific outcomes or solve all problems. Change takes time, commitment, and effort. Therapy is not a substitute for medical care, and if you are experiencing medical symptoms, please consult with a physician.

Your Consent

By scheduling a consultation with me, you are consenting to therapy as described in these terms. You have the right to ask questions about any of these policies. If you do not understand or disagree with any part of these terms, please let me know in our first session.

Questions about these terms?
Contact me at jennifer@consciouspractice.com or discuss in our first session.
