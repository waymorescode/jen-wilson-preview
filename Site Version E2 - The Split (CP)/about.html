About Jennifer

PhD, LCSW | St. Augustine Beach

I grew up knowing I wanted to understand people

From childhood, I was drawn to the quiet complexity of why people do what they do. That curiosity led me to psychology, then to research on developmental trauma and how early experiences shape the nervous system. My PhD focused on understanding the intergenerational patterns of trauma and resilience—how we carry forward what we've learned, both the wounds and the wisdom.

For years, I worked as the Director of Clinical Services at Vita Nova, where I trained clinicians, supervised complex cases, and collaborated with an interdisciplinary team in trauma-informed care. I've served as an expert witness in custody and competency evaluations. I've published research on clinical topics that matter.

But the work that changed me was always the direct clinical work—sitting across from someone who's ready to be honest, and holding space for the difficult and tender parts of their life. That's where therapy happens.

How I Work

Psychoanalytic Approaches

I draw from psychodynamic and relational therapy traditions—approaches that understand current struggles as connected to deeper patterns. We're not just managing symptoms; we're working toward genuine understanding.

Mindfulness & Nervous System Work

I integrate mindfulness practices and somatic approaches—work that helps your nervous system settle, so you can access your own wisdom and capacity for change.

Personal Investment

I'm not a blank slate. I bring genuine presence and care to every session. This isn't transactional. I'm invested in your process, your growth, and your freedom.

No Rush, No Labels

We move at your pace. I don't push toward a predetermined outcome or reduce you to a diagnosis. This is about understanding the full complexity of your experience.

Outside the Office

I'm a wife and mom. I live with my family in St. Augustine Beach, where the salt air and sunrise make sense of things that don't make sense anywhere else. I read widely—psychology, history, philosophy, fiction. I'm particular about my coffee. I think about people's stories constantly, the way we become who we are and the possibility of becoming different. I believe in therapy not as a luxury, but as a practice of paying attention to what matters most in our lives.
