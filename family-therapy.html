Family Therapy

Healing Family Relationships and Creating Healthier Patterns

When Family Feels More Like a Battlefield

The constant fighting. The slammed doors and silent treatment. The way everyone walks on eggshells. The guilt that you're failing as a parent or that your family is "broken." The exhaustion of trying to hold everything together while it keeps falling apart.

Family is supposed to be your safe place, but right now it feels like a war zone. Maybe your teenager is pulling away or acting out. Maybe your kids are struggling with your divorce. Maybe you're a blended family trying to figure out how to actually blend. Maybe trauma or mental health issues are affecting everyone.

Family therapy isn't about blaming anyone or declaring someone "the problem." It's about understanding how family patterns developed, improving communication, and creating a healthier system where everyone can thrive.

I Help Families With

Parent-Child Conflict

When you can't talk without fighting. When your child won't listen or respect boundaries. When you feel like you've lost your connection with your child. We'll work on improving communication, understanding each other's perspectives, and rebuilding trust and respect.

Behavioral Issues in Children & Teens

Acting out, defiance, aggression, risky behaviors—these are often symptoms of deeper issues. We'll address the underlying causes (trauma, anxiety, family dynamics) while also teaching parents effective strategies for managing behavior.

Blended Family Challenges

Stepparents, stepsiblings, ex-partners, different parenting styles—blending families is hard. We'll work on establishing new family roles, creating unified parenting approaches, managing loyalty conflicts, and building connection between step-relationships.

Divorce & Co-Parenting

How do you help your kids through divorce? How do you co-parent with someone you can't stand? We'll focus on minimizing harm to children, improving co-parenting communication, and helping kids adjust to their new reality.

Family Trauma

When trauma affects the whole family—death of a loved one, serious illness, abuse, natural disaster—everyone needs support. We'll process the trauma together and help your family heal as a unit.

Communication Breakdowns

When no one listens. When conversations turn into screaming matches. When family members shut down or storm off. I'll teach your family how to communicate effectively, express needs without attacking, and actually hear each other.

Sibling Conflict

Constant fighting, jealousy, favoritism, or one child being scapegoated. Sibling relationships set the foundation for future relationships. We'll address the conflicts and the family dynamics fueling them.

Major Life Transitions

Moving, new baby, job loss, illness, aging parents—major transitions stress families. We'll help your family navigate change together and use challenges to strengthen your bonds.

My Approach to Family Therapy

Systems Perspective

Families are systems—when one person changes, it affects everyone. I don't see one person as "the problem." Instead, we look at patterns, roles, and dynamics. Often the "problem child" is actually expressing what the whole family is feeling.

Everyone's Voice Matters

In family therapy, everyone gets heard—parents, kids, even young children (in age-appropriate ways). I create a safe space where family members can express themselves without fear of judgment or retaliation.

Teaching Communication Skills

Most family conflict stems from poor communication. I'll teach your family practical skills: active listening, expressing feelings without blame, problem-solving together, and repairing after conflicts.

Addressing Individual Needs

Sometimes family members need individual support too. I'll assess whether anyone needs individual therapy (for trauma, anxiety, depression) and coordinate care as needed.

Building on Strengths

Every family has strengths, even if they're hard to see right now. We'll identify what's working, what you love about each other, and build on those foundations.

When to Seek Family Therapy

Consider family therapy if:

  • Family members can't communicate without fighting
  • A child or teen is acting out, defiant, or struggling emotionally
  • You're going through a major transition (divorce, move, loss)
  • There's been trauma affecting the whole family
  • You're a blended family struggling to blend
  • Sibling conflict is constant and intense
  • One family member's mental health or addiction is affecting everyone
  • You feel disconnected as a family
  • Old patterns from your family of origin are repeating
  • You want to strengthen your family before problems develop

Don't wait until relationships are severely damaged. Early intervention makes healing easier.

What to Expect in Family Therapy

Who Attends?

Usually all family members living in the household, though this can vary. Sometimes I'll meet with parents alone first, or see a child individually. We'll figure out the best configuration for your family.

First Session: Assessment

I'll meet with your family to understand the challenges, family history, and goals. Everyone gets a chance to share their perspective. I'll also assess for safety concerns or individual issues needing attention.

Early Sessions: Building Foundation

We'll establish ground rules for respectful communication, identify patterns, and start teaching skills. You'll likely have "homework"—practicing new communication, family activities, or individual reflection.

Middle Phase: Deeper Work

As communication improves, we address deeper issues: past wounds, family-of-origin patterns, trauma, unspoken resentments. This phase can be challenging but is where real healing happens.

Later Sessions: Maintenance

Once you've made progress, sessions may become less frequent. We focus on maintaining gains and preparing your family to handle future challenges independently.

Timeline

Most families see improvement within 8-12 sessions, though complex issues may take longer. We'll check in regularly about progress and adjust as needed.

Common Questions

What if my child refuses to come?

We'll work on that together. Sometimes starting with just parents helps. Sometimes I can meet with the child individually first to build trust. Resistance often decreases once they see therapy isn't about blaming them.

Will you tell us how to parent?

I'll offer guidance and teach effective strategies, but I respect that you know your family best. My role is to support you, not judge you.

What if my ex won't participate?

Family therapy can still help even if one parent doesn't participate. We'll work with whoever is willing and able to attend.

How young is too young for family therapy?

Even young children (age 4+) can participate in family therapy in age-appropriate ways. I adjust my approach based on developmental level.

Your Family Deserves Peace

Imagine a home where people actually talk to each other. Where conflicts get resolved instead of escalating. Where everyone feels heard and valued. That's possible.
