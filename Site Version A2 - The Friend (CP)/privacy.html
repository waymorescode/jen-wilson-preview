    How We Protect Your Information

    Last Updated: October 2025

    Our Commitment to Your Privacy

    At Dr. Jennifer Wilson, protecting your privacy is fundamental to our practice. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your personal and health information in compliance with HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) and Florida state laws.

    HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices

    This notice describes how medical information about you may be used and disclosed and how you can get access to this information. Please review it carefully.

    Information We Collect

    In the course of providing therapy services, we may collect:

    • Contact information (name, address, phone, email)
    • Health insurance information
    • Medical and mental health history
    • Treatment notes and clinical records
    • Billing and payment information

    How We Use Your Information

    We use your protected health information for:

    • Treatment: Providing, coordinating, or managing your mental health care
    • Payment: Billing and collecting payment for services, including insurance claims
    • Healthcare Operations: Quality improvement, training, and business management

    When We May Disclose Your Information

    We will not disclose your information without your written authorization except in these situations:

    • Emergency situations: If you pose a serious danger to yourself or others
    • Child or elder abuse: If we have reasonable cause to suspect abuse or neglect
    • Court orders: When required by law or court order
    • Insurance billing: To your insurance company for payment purposes
    • Business associates: To vendors who help us operate (e.g., billing services), who are also bound by HIPAA

    Your Rights

    You have the following rights regarding your protected health information:

    • Right to Access: You may request to see or obtain copies of your records
    • Right to Amend: You may request corrections to your records
    • Right to Accounting: You may request a list of disclosures we've made
    • Right to Request Restrictions: You may request limits on how we use your information
    • Right to Confidential Communications: You may request we contact you in specific ways
    • Right to Revoke Authorization: You may revoke permission for us to use your information (except where already disclosed)

    Website Privacy

    Information Collected Through This Website

    When you visit our website, we may collect:

    • Information you voluntarily provide through contact forms
    • Basic usage data (pages visited, time spent, browser type)
    • Cookies for website functionality

    How We Use Website Information

    • To respond to your inquiries
    • To improve website functionality and user experience
    • To understand how visitors use our site

    Cookies

    Our website uses cookies to improve functionality. You can disable cookies in your browser settings, though some features may not work properly.

    Security Measures

    We implement appropriate physical, electronic, and administrative safeguards to protect your information, including:

    • Secure, encrypted storage of electronic records
    • Locked filing cabinets for paper records
    • Limited access to records (only authorized personnel)
    • Secure communication methods for telehealth
    • Regular security assessments and updates

    Telehealth Privacy

    For telehealth sessions, we use HIPAA-compliant video platforms with end-to-end encryption. However, please be aware that no electronic transmission is 100% secure. We recommend:

    • Using a private, secure location for sessions
    • Using a secure internet connection (not public WiFi)
    • Ensuring no one else can hear or see your session

    Third-Party Services

    We may use third-party services that are HIPAA-compliant, including:

    • Electronic health records systems
    • Billing and payment processors
    • Telehealth platforms
    • Insurance verification services (e.g., Headway)

    All third-party vendors sign Business Associate Agreements ensuring they protect your information according to HIPAA standards.

    Changes to This Policy

    We reserve the right to modify this Privacy Policy. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last Updated" date. We will notify you of significant changes.

    Questions or Concerns

    If you have questions about this Privacy Policy or believe your privacy rights have been violated, please contact:
