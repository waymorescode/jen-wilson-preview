    Your Right to Know Healthcare Costs

    Your Rights Under the No Surprises Act

    Under federal law (the No Surprises Act), healthcare providers must give uninsured or self-pay patients a Good Faith Estimate of expected charges before providing services.

    You have the right to receive a Good Faith Estimate for the total expected cost of any non-emergency healthcare services, including psychotherapy services.

    What is a Good Faith Estimate?

    A Good Faith Estimate shows the costs of items and services that are reasonably expected for your healthcare needs. The estimate is based on information known at the time it is created.

    The Good Faith Estimate does not include costs for items or services that may arise based on changes in your treatment plan or unexpected circumstances.

    Estimated Treatment Duration

    The length of therapy varies significantly based on individual needs and goals. Here are general estimates:

    • Short-term therapy (8-12 sessions): For specific, focused issues like recent stress, life transitions, or skill-building
    • Medium-term therapy (3-6 months): For anxiety, depression, relationship issues, or single-incident trauma
    • Longer-term therapy (6-12+ months): For complex trauma, eating disorders, or deep-rooted patterns

    Example Estimates

    Example 1: Short-term Individual Therapy

    • 1 Initial Assessment: $250
    • 11 Individual Sessions: $2,200 (11 × $200)
    • Total Estimated Cost: $2,450

    Example 2: Medium-term Individual Therapy (6 months)

    • 1 Initial Assessment: $250
    • 24 Individual Sessions (weekly for 6 months): $4,800 (24 × $200)
    • Total Estimated Cost: $5,050

    Example 3: Couples Therapy (3 months)

    • 1 Initial Assessment: $250
    • 12 Couples Sessions (weekly for 3 months): $2,700 (12 × $225)
    • Total Estimated Cost: $2,950

    Important Information

    This is an Estimate

    The actual cost of your therapy may differ from this estimate based on:

    • Changes in your treatment needs
    • Frequency of sessions (weekly, bi-weekly, monthly)
    • Length of treatment required
    • Whether you need extended sessions
    • Any additional services (letters, phone consultations, etc.)

    When You'll Receive Your Estimate

    You will receive a personalized Good Faith Estimate:

    • Before your first session, if you request one
    • After your initial assessment, when we can better estimate your treatment needs
    • Whenever your treatment plan significantly changes

    Insurance Clients

    If you are using insurance, you do not need a Good Faith Estimate. Your insurance company will provide an Explanation of Benefits (EOB) showing what you owe.

    Additional Fees

    The following may result in additional charges not included in session fees:

    • Late cancellations (less than 48 hours notice): Full session fee
    • No-shows: Full session fee
    • Letters or documentation: $50-150 depending on complexity
    • Phone consultations over 10 minutes: Prorated session rate

    Your Right to Dispute

    If you receive a bill that is at least $400 more than your Good Faith Estimate, you can dispute the bill. For more information about your right to a Good Faith Estimate and how to dispute a bill, visit www.cms.gov/nosurprises or call 1-800-985-3059.
