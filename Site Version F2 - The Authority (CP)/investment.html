Why Private Pay?

I maintain a private pay practice because it allows me to:

  • Keep my schedule and caseload small, so you have consistent access to me
  • Spend the time needed without insurance constraints or documentation pressures
  • Focus entirely on your care, not on insurance preauthorizations or session limits
  • Maintain complete confidentiality (no insurance company reviews or notifications beyond claims processing)
  • Work at a pace that serves your healing, not an insurance company's timeline

This approach allows for the depth and quality of care that creates real change.

Payment & Insurance

How It Works: You pay me directly for each session at the end of the appointment. I provide you with a superbill that you can submit to your insurance company for potential reimbursement.

Out-of-Network Reimbursement: Many insurance plans will cover 40-80% of out-of-network therapy costs. The amount depends on your specific plan and annual deductible. I recommend contacting your insurance company to ask about:

  • Out-of-network mental health benefits
  • Your deductible and how much you've already met
  • Percentage of coverage for outpatient therapy
  • Annual or session limits

Superbill Process: After each session, I provide an itemized invoice. You submit this to your insurance. They process the claim and reimburse you directly. I'm happy to answer questions about this process.

Payment Questions

Can we work out a different payment arrangement?

I typically work with private pay clients, but I'm open to conversation. If cost is a genuine barrier, let's discuss it during your consultation. I may have flexibility for the right fit.

What if I need to cancel a session?

I ask for 48 hours' notice for cancellations or rescheduling. If you cancel with less notice, I charge the full session fee. This policy respects both of our time and allows me to serve other clients who may need that slot. Exceptions are made for genuine emergencies.

Do you offer package discounts?

Yes. Intensive packages (4, 6, or 12 weeks) are structured for focused transformation and often include a discount compared to session-by-session rates. This is discussed during your consultation based on what you're working toward.

What if my insurance doesn't cover much?

This is a real consideration. Some plans have high deductibles or limited out-of-network benefits. That said, many clients find that investing in their mental health is an investment in everything else—their relationships, their work, their wellbeing. We can talk through this during your consultation and explore what makes sense for your situation.
