About Jennifer

PhD, LCSW | St. Augustine Beach

I grew up knowing I wanted to understand people.

From my earliest days, I was curious about what drives people, how they heal, what gets in the way of real connection. That curiosity shaped everything that came after.

I earned my PhD in Developmental Psychology, with a dissertation focused on early trauma and its lasting impact on attachment patterns. That research—alongside thousands of clinical hours—showed me that most people's struggles aren't about being broken. They're about disconnection. From themselves. From others. From what matters.

I spent years as Clinical Director at Vita Nova Palm Beach, training therapists, conducting clinical supervision, and working directly with clients navigating life's heaviest moments. I've testified as an expert witness in custody matters. Published research on trauma-informed care. Taught at the university level. And through it all, I've built a practice rooted in one core belief: real change happens in the space between how things look and how things feel.

How I Work

My approach blends psychoanalytic depth with mindfulness-informed practice. I believe therapy works best when it's not rushed, not labeled, and deeply personal.

I don't offer quick fixes. I offer presence. The kind of listening that makes space for what you've never said out loud. The kind of work that respects both your ambition and your humanity.

Sessions are tailored to you—whether that's individual work, couples therapy, or an intensive package designed for focused transformation. I meet you exactly where you are, not where you think you should be.

Outside the Office

I'm deeply rooted in Northeast Florida. My husband and I raised our son here. We're ocean people—you'll find us on the beach at sunrise, reading widely, exploring what makes people and communities thrive.

I believe in the power of community connection, both in practice and in life. When I'm not with clients, I'm thinking about what brings people together, how we build belonging, and what it means to show up authentically in a world that often demands the opposite.
