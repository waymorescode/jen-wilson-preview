About Jennifer

PhD, LCSW | St. Augustine Beach

I grew up knowing I wanted to understand people

Like, deeply. I was the friend everyone talked to about their stuff, the one who actually listened instead of just waiting for my turn to talk. That curiosity turned into a career.

I did my PhD research on something heavy — the developmental impact of trauma, specifically for kids who experienced abuse early. That sounds academic, but it wasn't really. It was about understanding how our earliest experiences shape us, and then figuring out what actually helps people heal and move forward. I've spent thousands of hours in the room with people working through their stuff, and I taught that material to graduate students and therapists-in-training.

Most recently, I was Director of Clinical Services at Vita Nova in Palm Beach, working with at-risk young people. Before that, I taught at three different universities and consulted for nonprofits. I've been qualified as an expert witness in court cases, published research in peer-reviewed journals, and trained other therapists on how to do the deeper work.

But here's what really matters: I actually care about the people I work with. I show up with real attention. I notice what's underneath what you're saying. And I don't pretend to have all the answers — I'm genuinely curious about your life and what would actually make it better.

How I Work

Therapy in my office is different than you might expect. It's deeper and slower than a lot of what's out there. I use psychoanalytic approaches — which basically means we're getting at the roots, not just managing the symptoms. I weave in mindfulness and self-care throughout, because how you move through the world matters, not just what you think about it.

I'm also someone who believes in personal investment. You're not a file to me. You're a person I'm working with, and I take that seriously.

My Credentials

I'm a Licensed Clinical Social Worker (LCSW #8431 in Florida) and a Qualified Supervisor by the Florida Board of Clinical Social Work. I have over 5,000 clinical hours. I have a PhD in clinical social work with a focus on developmental trauma. I'm a Cornell University Summer Research Institute alumna. I've published in peer-reviewed journals and contributed book chapters.

But honestly? The credential that matters most is that people feel better after working with me, and they trust me to know what I'm doing.

Outside the Office

I live here with my husband and our son. We're part of this community — not just practicing in it. You might see me at the beach or grabbing coffee. My kid's probably in school with your kid. I'm invested in this place the way someone is invested in a place they actually live.
