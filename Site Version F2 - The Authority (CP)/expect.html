What to Expect

How our work together unfolds

The Consultation

This is where we start. You'll schedule a 30-minute consultation call — no cost, no obligation. We'll talk about what brought you, what you're hoping for, and whether working together makes sense. I'll answer questions. You'll get a sense of how I work.

Your First Session

We'll have about 50 minutes together. I'll ask you about what's happening now, what you're noticing, what you want to change. I'm not following a checklist. I'm listening for patterns, for the shape of your particular situation.

By the end of the first session, you'll know whether this feels right. I will have heard you. You'll have started to articulate something that's been on the edge of language. That's the beginning.

How Sessions Work

Most people come weekly. Some come bi-weekly. It depends on what you need and what makes sense for your life. Sessions are 50 minutes. They're held in my office in St. Augustine Beach, and I also offer virtual sessions if that works better for you.

Between sessions, you might notice things. You might have a difficult conversation and think differently about it. You might feel something shift. That's the work — it doesn't all happen in the room.

The Approach

I'm not here to tell you what to do. I'm here to help you think differently — about your patterns, your relationships, yourself. Sometimes that's gentle. Sometimes that's a careful challenge.

I pay attention to what's underneath the surface: the beliefs you don't know you're holding, the ways you've adapted to what happened to you, the patterns that made sense once but might not anymore.

I also believe in the nervous system. If you're overwhelmed or dysregulated, the best insight in the world won't land. So we'll work with your whole self — your thoughts, your body, your emotions, the space between us.

What Changes Look Like

It's rarely linear. You'll have clarity and then confusion again. You'll shift something and then find the same pattern in a different context. That's not failure — that's learning.

Real change is usually quiet. You notice you respond differently to something that always triggered you. A conversation with your partner lands differently. You stop carrying something you've been carrying for years. You feel more like yourself.

It takes time. But by the time you're six months in, the difference is usually visible.

The first step is a consultation call. Let's talk about what might work for you.
